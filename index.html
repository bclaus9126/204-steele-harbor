My honest read on what happened

The price moved around a lot, and buyers read that as uncertainty+

Over the run, the asking number went up and down more than once. To a buyer watching listings, a price that keeps shifting signals that even the seller is not sure what the home is worth, so they wait to see where it lands instead of acting. A steady, confident number that matches what the area supports does the opposite. It tells buyers the price is real and gives them a reason to move now rather than wait.

Late in the run the number went up instead of getting sharper+

After a long stretch without a sale, the asking price ended up higher than where it had been earlier, rather than tightening to meet the market. When a home has already been sitting, buyers expect the next move to be sharper and more competitive, not a step up. Raising into a slow market usually adds resistance instead of value. The fix is to set one correct number and let it do the work, rather than testing higher after the market has already spoken.

A new home competes head to head with the builder+

This is the big one for a brand-new resale. A buyer shopping this community is also sitting across from the builder, and the builder can hand out rate buydowns, closing cost help, and a fresh warranty that a private seller usually cannot match dollar for dollar. The way to win is not to out-discount them. It is to be clearly the better deal: ready to move in now, with upgrades, blinds, and landscaping already in, and no build wait. That edge has to be made obvious in the price and the marketing, not assumed.

One clear price, held, and a clean relaunch+

A brand-new home that has sat for a while makes buyers wonder why, even when the answer is just the price story above. The cleanest reset is a new listing number and one correct price from day one, held steady instead of nudged around. Pair that with a sharp first photo and a marketing push aimed at buyers who want new construction without the wait, and you get fresh eyes from people who scrolled past the older listing.
